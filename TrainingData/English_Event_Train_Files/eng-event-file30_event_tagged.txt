 <PLACE-ARG ID="2"><LINK EVENT_ARG="3" ID="11" TYPE_REL="EVENT_ARG"/> SHIMLA: </PLACE-ARG> A mock drill on <NATURAL_EVENT ID="3" TYPE="EARTHQUAKE"> earthquake </NATURAL_EVENT> was conducted in all 12 districts of <PLACE-ARG ID="4"><LINK EVENT_ARG="3" ID="12" TYPE_REL="EVENT_ARG"/> Himachal Pradesh </PLACE-ARG><TIME-ARG ID="5"><LINK EVENT_ARG="3" ID="13" TYPE_REL="EVENT_ARG"/> on Thursday </TIME-ARG> in which representatives from the Army and the Indian Air Force from Western Command took part. Teams from Army, paramilitary forces, ITBP, NDRF also participated in the mock drill along with police, medical, fire services, civil defence, home guards. 

 Himachal is prone to various hazards, both natural and man-made. However, the hazard which poses biggest threat to the state is earthquake. According to state government data information, the state has been struck by <NATURAL_EVENT ID="8" TYPE="EARTHQUAKE"> earthquakes </NATURAL_EVENT> nearly 120 times with <MAGNITUDE-ARG ID="9"><LINK EVENT_ARG="8" ID="14" TYPE_REL="EVENT_ARG"/> magnitude of 4.0 </MAGNITUDE-ARG> and above on the Richter scale. 

 As per the Bureau of Indian Standard (BIS) seismic zoning map, 5 districts of the state — Chamba (53.2%), Hamirpur (90.9%), Kangra (98.6%), Kullu (53.1%), Mandi (97.4%) — have 53 to 98.6% of their area liable to Medvedev–Sponheuer–Karnik (MSK) IX or more. The remaining area of these districts are liable to MSK VIII. 

 Medvedev–Sponheuer–Karnik (MSK), is a macroseismic intensity scale used to evaluate the severity of ground shaking on the basis of observed effects in an area of the earthquake occurrence 

 Two districts, Bilaspur (25.3%) and Una (37.0%) have substantial area in MSK IX and rest in MSK VIII.
